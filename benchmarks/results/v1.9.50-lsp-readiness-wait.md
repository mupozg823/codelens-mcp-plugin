# P0-4 LSP readiness wait-for-ready (v1.9.50, 2026-04-20)

Replaces the fixed 45 s `sleep` that followed
`prepare_harness_session`'s auto-attach prewarm with a readiness-poll
loop. The bench now asks CodeLens whether the pooled LSP sessions
have handshaked (and, optionally, returned at least one non-empty
result) and returns as soon as the answer is yes — or fails fast when
the LSP failed to start at all.

## Mechanism

**Engine (`lsp::readiness`)**

Each `LspSessionPool` entry carries an `Arc<ReadinessState>` with
lock-free atomic counters:

- `started_at` — session spawn instant.
- `ms_to_first_response` — latches on the first `Ok` round-trip
  (handshake + any LSP method succeeded).
- `ms_to_first_nonempty` — latches on the first `Ok` response whose
  body was non-empty.
- `response_count` / `nonempty_count` / `failure_count` — rolling
  diagnostics.

Pool method `readiness_snapshot()` returns one row per session.
Rows persist across spawn failures: `get_or_start_session` inserts
the readiness row _before_ calling `LspSession::start`, so a session
that never initialises still leaves a `failure_count > 0, is_alive =
false` row for the poller to see.

**MCP (`get_lsp_readiness`)**

New read-only handler that returns the pool snapshot plus three
aggregate flags:

- `all_alive` — every pooled session's `is_alive` is true.
- `all_ready` — every pooled session's `is_ready` (nonempty latch) is
  true.
- `any_ready` — at least one session flipped its nonempty latch.

Aggregates are conservatively `false` when `session_count == 0`, so
pollers treat an empty pool as "not ready yet" rather than "nothing
to wait for".

**Bench (`lsp-boost-http-matrix.py --wait-for-ready`)**

Polls `get_lsp_readiness` every `--ready-poll-interval-ms` (default
500 ms) up to `--auto-attach-wait` seconds. Terminates when:

- `all_ready` flips (strong signal), **or**
- `all_alive` flips AND `--ready-grace-seconds` (default 5) have
  elapsed since the alive transition.

The grace window is the practical hedge: pyright and
`typescript-language-server` answer empty-query `workspace/symbol`
prewarms with `[]`, so the nonempty-latch rarely flips from auto-
attach alone. Alive + grace is the shippable ready signal.

## Results — three repos

Settings: `--auto-attach-wait 60` upper bound, `--wait-for-ready` on,
grace values per repo.

| Repo  | Grace | Alive at | Returned at | Elapsed    | vs 60 s blind | LSP sessions                          |
| ----- | ----- | -------- | ----------- | ---------- | ------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| flask | 5 s   | 0.50 s   | 5.55 s      | **5.6 s**  | **10.8×**     | pyright alive                         |
| self  | 15 s  | 0.51 s   | 15.64 s     | **15.6 s** | **3.8×**      | pyright + rust-analyzer alive         |
| zod   | 10 s  | —        | —           | 60.0 s     | 1.0×          | tsserver failure_count=1, alive=false |

For flask / self the wait-for-ready path proves dramatically shorter
than the blind sleep while preserving the MRR / bucket-hit numbers
from the §1c stratified matrix:

- flask thick_caller **3/3**, thin_wrapper 5/10
- self thick_caller **5/5**, thin_wrapper 6/10
- zod thick_caller **3/3** (via tree-sitter fallback; tsserver is
  broken on this repo for a reason separate from P0-4)

## What the zod row tells us

`typescript-language-server` failed to initialise (`failure_count=1,
is_alive=false`). Pre-P0-4 this would have been invisible: the bench
would have slept 45 s, the arm would have silently used the tree-
sitter union path, and no diagnostic would have been emitted. With
readiness visible, the failure is surfaced the moment it happens,
and callers can choose to abort, fall back, or alert.

The `--auto-attach-wait 60` upper bound still protects against a
genuinely stuck handshake — the readiness poll never waits longer
than the user asked it to.

## Honest limits

- `is_ready` is a strong claim (first nonempty response); the
  practical readiness signal the bench actually uses is
  `is_alive + grace_seconds`. The stronger latch is retained for
  callers that can afford to wait for it (e.g. rust-analyzer
  warming returns a populated workspace-symbol result within a few
  seconds of `all_alive`).
- The grace window is language-specific in practice (pyright `~5s`,
  rust-analyzer `~15s`, tsserver `~10s` on small repos). The bench
  exposes it as a CLI knob rather than hard-coding per language, so
  repos with unusually large projects can tune without touching the
  harness.
- Dead-LSP detection waits out the full `--auto-attach-wait` window
  today. A future pass could short-circuit on `failure_count > 0,
is_alive = false` after a short diagnosis delay, but that trades
  off against transient spawn flakes.
