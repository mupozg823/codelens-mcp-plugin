# P1-4 per-ref weighting matrix (v1.9.50, 2026-04-20)

Follow-up to the 3-repo containment measurement
(`v1.9.50-lsp-boost-matrix-3repo.md`). `lsp_proximity_factor` now
scales the containment branch by `count(refs ∈ span) /
LSP_CONTAINMENT_SATURATION_REFS` (saturation = 3), and caps the
outside-span decay at `LSP_OUTSIDE_SPAN_MAX_FACTOR = 0.30` so that
any genuine container outranks any mere neighbour regardless of how
tight the neighbour sits.

## Headline

| Repo  | Baseline         | Containment (prev) | Per-ref (new)    | ΔMRR vs baseline |
| ----- | ---------------- | ------------------ | ---------------- | ---------------- |
| self  | 1/10, MRR 0.0500 | 8/10, MRR 0.1145   | 7/10, MRR 0.1264 | **+153%**        |
| flask | 2/10, MRR 0.0500 | 2/10, MRR 0.0444   | 3/10, MRR 0.0553 | **+11%**         |
| zod   | 4/10, MRR 0.0932 | 4/10, MRR 0.0703   | 4/10, MRR 0.0713 | −23%             |

Flask has crossed from **−11% → +11%** versus baseline — the
per-ref knob does the thing it was introduced to do. Self keeps its
≥+150% dominance; zod stays stuck (dataset-driven, see below).

## What changed from the containment run

- Inside span: factor is `min(count / 3, 1.0)`. A function with one
  ref earns `0.333`; with three or more it saturates at `1.0`.
- Outside span: factor is the old linear decay times `0.30`. The
  cap keeps outside factors strictly below inside-single (0.333 >
  max outside 0.30), so _any_ container beats _any_ neighbour.
- Two new engine tests pin the contract:
  `lsp_signal_multi_ref_container_beats_single_ref_container` and
  the reshaped `lsp_signal_proximity_prefers_nearer_ref_lines`.
  Engine 290 → 291, MCP `--features http` 499/499.

## Why self slipped one hit (8 → 7) but gained MRR

Two queries that previously clung to rank 21–23 fell off the
top-K: `RankingContext` and `default_lsp_command_for_path`. Both
expected callers are single-ref containers in files where
neighbouring symbols also satisfy containment, so under the old
1.0-flat scheme the expected symbol shared the ceiling and was
dragged up by the chaff; under per-ref it correctly lands at
`0.333` while multi-ref containers climb to `1.0`, and it loses the
tie-break to them.

The gain comes from the queries that still hit — they now rank
dramatically higher (e.g. `find_referencing_symbols_via_lsp`
5 → 2, `LspRequest` 9 → 5). That shift is why MRR rose from 0.1145
to 0.1264 despite dropping one hit.

## Why flask turned positive

Flask's ref-dense `helpers.py` / `sansio/*.py` used to tie every
containing symbol at 1.0; per-ref now hands the top score to the
function enclosing the most references to the probe target, which
happens to be the expected caller in `Scaffold → App`. `Scaffold`
moved from _no hit_ → rank 3.

## Why zod still regresses

Zod's dataset is thin by construction: the expected callers
(e.g. `_parse → parse`, `registry → globalRegistry`) are tiny
wrappers that contain exactly one ref to the probe target, while
other symbols in the same file enclose three or more and saturate
at `1.0`. The signal is pointed at the wrong winner for this
dataset — per-ref is correct under its definition; the definition
is the wrong one for "find the thin wrapper".

Follow-up to unstick zod: augment the dataset with "thick caller"
expectations, or layer a text-similarity tiebreaker on top of the
containment factor.
